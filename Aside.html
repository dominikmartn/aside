Aside 1.4.4

A stability release: the mid-meeting crash is fixed, a crashed meeting now recovers itself, and the transcript keeps to one language.

Fixed

  • The mid-meeting crash is fixed. A race in the live transcript could corrupt memory and take the whole app down while you were recording. Thanks for the crash reports, they led straight to it.
  • Crashed meetings recover themselves. If Aside ever quits during a recording, the next launch rebuilds the note on its own: the library opens on the recovering meeting, and it lands in your list with a Recovered badge. No more hidden recovery offer to find.
  • The transcript no longer flips languages. Aside now detects the meeting's language once at the start and sticks to it, instead of re-guessing every few seconds and jumping between German and English mid-meeting.
  • First launch transcribes correctly. The starter model was paired with the wrong tokenizer and produced garbage until the full model finished downloading. Fresh installs transcribe properly from the first meeting.
  • Recordings can't silently vanish. If saving the audio fails, or a crashed meeting can't be read back, Aside now keeps the backup and tells you, instead of quietly dropping the recording.
  • You'll know if only your mic is captured. When the call audio drops mid-meeting (for example switching to AirPods), Aside now warns you it went mic-only, instead of recording just you for the rest of the call without a word.
  • Chat no longer garbles. Asking a follow-up while the previous answer is still streaming used to scramble that answer. It doesn't anymore.
  • Fewer crashes. Closing the lid or unplugging a monitor mid-recording, and a few odd transcript cases, no longer crash the app.
  • Errors are actually visible. When a recording won't start or a note won't save, you now see why, instead of it failing behind a hidden window.

Improved

  • Chat answers are formatted. Headings, bullets and bold in the copilot's replies now render properly instead of showing raw markup.
  • Copy copies what you're looking at. On the Notes tab it copies the notes; on the Transcript tab it copies the transcript.
  • The live transcript follows along. It scrolls with the conversation, and if you scroll up to read back, it stays put instead of yanking you down.
  • Regenerate a missing summary. If a meeting saved without a summary because the copilot was unreachable, you can generate it later from the saved transcript.
  • The pin is clearer. It fills in when the panel is held open, so the state is obvious at a glance.

Privacy

  • An honest explanation of your data. Audio and transcription stay on your Mac; transcript text is sent only to the AI you pick, to write summaries and answer questions. The privacy page now says exactly this.
  • Hardened. Meeting text can no longer try to hijack the copilot, and your notes and recordings are stored with tighter file permissions.

Anonymous usage counts (never your content) help guide what gets built next, with an off switch in Settings. Crash reports can be shared too, opt-in and technical only, never audio or transcripts.
